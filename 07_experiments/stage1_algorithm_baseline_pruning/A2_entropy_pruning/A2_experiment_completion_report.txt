Main conclusion:
Layer 21 is the entropy-lowest fusion-refinement layer, but its activation footprint is the smallest among the measured layers. Layer 15 has the largest activation footprint, but its entropy is not low. Layer 18 provides the best first candidate for hardware-aware entropy-guided pruning because it combines relatively low entropy with a non-trivial feature-map movement cost.

Recommended pruning candidate:
Primary candidate: layer 18

Control candidates:
Entropy-lowest control: layer 21
Hardware-cost-highest control: layer 15

Not recommended as first candidate:
layer 12

Experiment boundary:
This experiment completed entropy measurement and candidate selection only. It did not modify the YOLO model, prune channels, remove branches, or perform fine-tuning.

Paper-ready result statement:
The feature entropy analysis over 64 validation images shows that layer 21 has the lowest average spatial entropy, while layer 15 has the largest activation footprint. Considering both information entropy and activation movement cost, layer 18 is selected as the primary hardware-aware entropy-guided pruning candidate for the next ablation experiment.
